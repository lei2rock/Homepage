About me

My CV (PDF)

I hold both a master’s and a bachelor’s degree in economics and have gained valuable research experience through my participation in a Ph.D. program in business economics. My research primarily focuses on the implementation of antimonopoly law, market power in the trade of carbon emission allowances, as well as public policy in city planning.

Through training in various research projects, I have developed professional expertise in data analysis and economic modeling. I have a particular focus on conducting empirical analysis using panel and time series data through statistical software such as STATA and R. My skills in these areas allow me to effectively analyze complex data sets and draw meaningful conclusions.

In my work, I strive to be objective, meticulous, and thorough to ensure that every task achieves the best results. I am dedicated to delivering high-quality work and am always looking for ways to improve my skills and knowledge. Additionally, my experience as a part-time teaching assistant in Hong Kong for several years has enabled me to develop strong communication skills and the ability to effectively convey complex concepts to students of all levels.
